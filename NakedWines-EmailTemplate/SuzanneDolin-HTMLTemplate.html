Marcelo’s family has grown grapes in Mendoza for generations. He grew up in the family vineyards, so knows them like the back of his hand. As a child he learned from his father and grandfather how to grow the grapes with care and tenderness. That’s a priceless asset for a winemaker.

Back then they had to sell them to be blended into table wines.

“It was a pity, for such profound care we gave to them. I watched in silence since I was little - that made an impact on my character, on my senses, and in my life.”

Marcelo never forgot that experience. So in 2001 he set up his own modest little winery, on a mission to produce quality wines that did justice to his ancestors’ grapes.“Our mission was to become Mendoza’s iconic winery for the excellence of our wines.”

He believes passionately that each bottle should taste of the grape varieties and terroir it’s from, not some human jiggery-pokery in the winery.

“This is my idea of how a true Malbec should be. This means the smallest human interaction or correction. Organic agriculture and meticulous care of the grapes. Fruit driven wine, striving for varietal purity and elegance, and an expression of terroir, but avoiding over-concentration or heavy use of oak.”


3 reasons why Marcelo’s Malbec stands out from the rest, making him our highest-rated Malbec winemaker:


  • First, he has vineyards in Mendoza’s oldest Malbec fine wine regions, with vines from 10 to 100 years old. They’re also some of the highest altitude vineyards in Mendoza. So hot sunny days and super-cool nights give the grapes a naturally higher concentration of colour, aromas and flavour, and the wines a greater longevity.

  • Secondly he hand-harvests and manually-selects the grapes for maximum concentration of flavour, aroma and elegance of tannins. That way he doesn’t have to ‘over extract’ or ‘over oak’ the grapes to artificially add ‘oomph’.

  • Finally Marcelo ages the wines minimally in French oak barrels specially chosen to enhance the aromas and flavours of each grape variety, followed by a long bottling period.
